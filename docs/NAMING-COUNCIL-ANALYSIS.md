## ADVISOR RESPONSES (13 Independent Perspectives)

### **1. THE CONTRARIAN**

StructureWalk is a liability. "Structure" is cold, clinical. Patients are anxious—they want *hope*, not scaffolding. SymptomScape is worse: symptom + landscape? It's a mouthful, and "symptom" is the *problem*, not the solution. 

The real winners are taken (Clarity, Recall, Anchor). Why? Because they're *short*, *human*, *memorable*. You're settling for mediocre domains because you want cheap branding. That's backwards. Pay USD 2K for Clarity.com if it's available, and own the market. Settling for StructureWalk is like settling for AeroSweep when you could have had Clearsweep.

**Recommendation:** Don't launch with either. Buy the best single-word .com available (even if pricey). If forced: StructureWalk > SymptomScape (at least it's a verb).

---

### **2. THE FIRST PRINCIPLES THINKER**

You're asking the wrong question. "What should we name it?" assumes the name is the brand. It's not.

The brand is: *structured ADHD preparation that saves clinicians 45 minutes and patients anxiety.*

The name should *reflect* that, not *be* that.

StructureWalk: "Structure" + "Walk" = procedural journey. Actually good metaphor. Patient walks through structured interview.

SymptomScape: "Symptom" + "scape" = landscape to navigate. Also decent metaphor.

But both are clunky. Better question: *What's the patient's emotional journey?* 
- Anxiety → Clarity
- Confusion → Structure (or Map, or Compass)
- Overwhelm → Prepare, Ready, Set

Recommend you pick based on *emotional outcome*, not domain availability. Then buy the .com. Domains are cheap; brand is expensive.

**Recommendation:** Pick Clarity or Prepare, regardless of domain. Buy .com at any price.

---

### **3. THE EXPANSIONIST**

StructureWalk and SymptomScape are *narrow*. They're ADHD-specific. But you said the vision is *multi-condition* (autism, bipolar, PTSD by Year 2+).

A name locked to "symptoms" or "structure" pigeonholes you. In Year 2, when you launch the bipolar module, "SymptomScape for Bipolar" sounds forced.

Better: Pick a name that *scales*.
- Clarity (works for any condition)
- Parse (works for any complex data)
- Prepare (works for any assessment)
- Anchor (works as a metaphor for stability across conditions)

If those are taken, invent a *platform name* that's condition-agnostic:
- Diagnostic Flow
- Assessment Hub
- Clinical Prep
- Clinician Bridge

Or go with Clarity Diagnostics (brand name) vs. Clarity (product).

**Recommendation:** Don't name it ADHD-first. Name it assessment-first. StructureWalk is a missed opportunity.

### **7. THE SKEPTIC**

Both available options have fatal flaws:

**StructureWalk:**
- Two-word compound = harder to remember
- "Walk" implies movement, journey, process (good metaphor, bad clarity)
- Risk: Patients confuse it with fitness app
- Risk: Clinicians don't take it seriously (sounds too casual)

**SymptomScape:**
- Weird neologism (symptom + landscape)
- "Symptom" is negative framing (why name a service after the *problem*, not the *solution*?)
- "Scape" is trendy but will date badly (remember Friendster? Namescape? Exactly.)
- Risk: Clinicians think it's a symptom *tracker* (which it's not)

Both are mediocre compromises. If you ship StructureWalk or SymptomScape, you'll spend 2 years explaining what the product is. That's debt.

**Real question:** Why are all the good names taken? Answer: They're good.

**Recommendation:** Buy Clarity or Anchor (cost be damned). Or invent a *new* name that's available *and* good. (That's hard, but possible.)
